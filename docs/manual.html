Areas

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

Currently, you can only travel between zones using the Pi Gate. In the final game, there will be Pi Gates around but there world will be far more open and foot travely.

Lobi Town

Lobi Town is the starting zone. Towns follow a different ruleset than fields. For now, the only differences are :

  • Equipment does not lose durability/uses
  • Inventory is wiped upon entering but not exiting

In the future :

  • Items will be cashed in for bits upon entering
  • There will be vendors and special town-only services
  • Other reasonably expected limitations

Test Room

test_room is an endurance room. While you stay there, two target dummies will spawn every 10 seconds, up to a limit of 6. If you see more than 6, please report the conditions upon which this happened. Most likely, it means sub_host_migration() is broken again. There is a safe place in the room, if you look hard enough.

The white circle in the test_room is what's called a Breakpoint. Breakpoints will appear throughout the final game as points of management. You'll be able to change your defaults, fast travel, check logs, and access the hacking menu from these.

Test Room 2

test_room2 is a hallway designed to give the bros a brief feeling of what it might be like to run a dungeon together. You'll have to search around for the exit.

Dungeons in the final game will be randomly placed on the overworld. They'll have semi-random insides. They will be separate zones from their entrances entirely. So, on the overworld there's a little witch hut. The inside might be a 100 floor tower. The goal will always be to find the mcguffin.

Combat Mechanics

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

The combat mechanics are not final, even in design. They will take much iteration to get right and I hope you'll help with that.

Damage

Currently, all attacks do 1 damage. The capacity for typed damage exists but is not really used at the moment.

HP

You have 4 HP. You have a health meter in the top left. You will heal when you switch rooms for now. Enemies also have 4HP.

In the future, healing will take place at camp sites and regularly timed intervals. There will be many sources of temporary HP.


Anatomy of a Strike

When an attack lands, this is the series of checks that takes place. They only take place on the first thing an attack collides with.

Was I blocked or parried? > Am I unblockable? > Did I hit something that was receiving hits? > I've identified the thing I hit > What direction did I enter their hitbox from? > Is there a non-default equipment in the related equipment slot?

If yes, then we reduce the durability (also remaining uses) of that item by 1. If no, we take an HP equivalent to the damage of the strike (1 in this version).

What this boils down to is that equipment (but not defaults) will protect you from attacks at the cost of their durability. Hats will defend overheads. Offhands will protect left-hand strikes. Mainhands protect right-hand and blocking. Boots protect feet.

Blocking & Parrying

Currently, blocking ignores all non-unblockable strikes from the front. There should be some stop or stagger on a successful block but there is currently not. There is a large hitbox covering from about the knees to a little over the head. If you get struck from behind while blocking, you will still take damage. Some strikes are a little glitchy and may start inside you, resulting in a bad hit. Sorry about that.

Parrying does not use the same hitbox as blocking. It disables the front blocking box and enables other directional boxes of about the same size on each side of the character. However, a successful parry will result in a staggered enemy.

So, if you parry left, you can still get dickstabbed. In all cases, your feet are technically unprotected. This is intentional and you should jump. I believe the only low attack right now (club_combo_1) has too big of a hitbox to go under the blocking box. In the future, shockwaves and such will require a jump.

Defaults and Equipment

Defaults are the equipment you start the game with. They do not lose durability and do not protect against incoming attacks. However, they also have infinite uses. When your equipment breaks, these are the items that you will return to automatically. They can only be selected in the main menu or town. In the final game, you'll be able to customize them at camp as well. Choose the defaults with the looks and abilities you'd like to always have access to, should things get dicey.

Equipment are the items you equip from the item menu during normal play. These items will provide armor. These items have limited uses (outside town, feel free to experiment inside town). Uses and durability are the same number. Running out of either one will destroy the item. A destroyed equipment results in a short stagger animation that will prevent you from acting. Keep this in mind when using your final Fading Horizon on your katana. If it doesn't slay, you might get got.

Defaults are permanently unlocked (currently everything is unlocked). Equipment are temporary. They'll be consumed on use and wiped entirely when you return to town. In the final game, this wipe will result in currency/score that you can use to buy cool gear for your next outing. It is intentional that you start each run with limited options.

What's Unblockable?

Currently, all strong attacks are unblockable. In the final game, they'll have an interaction with armored zones instead. Dash Punch cannot be staggered and is unblockable. You will still take damage. Lick is unblockable.

Controls

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

Currently, all gameplay functions should be supported by both controller and keyboard. Menus are generally going to be mouse and keyboard only. Chat is keyboard only. Testing is generally done with mouse and keyboard, although the final game will favor controllers.

Keyboard


Universal

  • Movement - WASD
  • Camera - Mouse
  • Draw/Sheathe Weapon - Tab
  • Jump - Space
  • Item Menu - Hold 2 on the toprow
  • Cycle Item - Mouse Wheel
  • Equip Item - Middle Mouse
  • Interact - E
  • Console/Chat - `
  • Activate Item Slot:
    • Head - v
    • Mainhand - c
    • Boots - x
    • Offhand - z
  • Destroy Item Slot:
    • Head - Alt + v
    • Mainhand - Alt + c
    • Boots - Alt + x
    • Offhand - Alt + z

Weapon Drawn

  • Light Attack - Left Click
  • Strong Attack - E
  • Guard - Right Click
  • Parry - Held Right Click + WASD
  • Quickstep - WASD + LShift
  • Dash - Right Click during Forward Quickstep
  • Dash Attack - Left Click during Dash
  • Falling Attack - Left Click during descent
  • Change Lock Target - Q

Out of Combat

  • Sprint - LShift

In Combat

  • Change Lock Target - Q

    • Boots - L2 + X

  • Destroy Item Slot:
    • Head - R2 + /_\
    • Mainhand - R2 + O

Creating a Character

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

On the Main Menu, you'll see 3 buttons and a text box.

  • Offline - Host a local server with a player limit of 1
  • Online
    • Join - Connect to the hardlinked Crabsoft HQ server
    • Host - Create a local server with a player limit of 4
  • Set Defaults - Customize before loading in
  • Text Box - Whatever name is in this box is the local character data that will be loaded

Set Defaults

See the image below for visuals.

  1. This currently shows the raw data of the save file. Later on it'll be formatted to be more selective
  2. This is the character preview, as you select items they'll appear here
  3. You can select your overhead system message color here (the color of item pickup notices)
  4. You can select your overhead chat color here (other players will see it)
  5. You can set your character name here. This will be used to index your save file and as your name in chat with other players.
  6. By clicking on an Item Icon, you can select your defaults. This shows the name, a little flavor text, and the active ability that goes along with it. Click to select, click again or another to deselect.
    1. Chest, Legs, and Gloves do not actually grant abilities. They are purely cosmetic. Eventually they'll grant some stats but still no active.
  7. If you click Exit, nothing will be saved.
  8. If you click Save, your choices will be immediately committed to a save file by the same name as the current character name entry. You may have as many saves as you want. Using the same name twice will overwrite existing data.

Once you've made all your selections, click save and join up! You'll spawn into Lobi Town.

Feedback

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

I'll be opening a Discord channel for the 7/1 test. This is the best place to dump feedback from that specific version of the game.

I know that many of you are shy! Feel free to DM anything on Discord, anytime.

What is good feedback?

For feedback to be helpful, it needs to be about your specific experience. It has to talk about how you felt, your outcomes of playing the game. Too often, folks give feedback based on what they imagine I might be going for or what "people" are into. None of that is actually helpful. You're the one playing. You're the one testing. I need to know how it was for you and I'll do all the filtering and grains of salt later.

Feedback has to be followed-up. If you test the game today and tell me an animation felt slow, that gives me something to go off. However, I can't know that it no longer feels slow unless you experience the new animation. Half fixes are infintely worse than doing nothing. Please do try to make yourself available for follow-ups.


What is worth feedbacking?

I want to hear every single thought that occurs to you while playing my game. If you thought it was weird that you couldn't go somewhere, I need to know. If you expected to be able to sit in a chair and couldn't, I need that too. If you really wish that club_combo_3 was faster so that you could combine it with another thing to do something cool, that's fantastic food for thought. Wonky controls, off colors, or even names you just don't like. Nothing is out of bounds or too minor if it's your honest reaction to the game.


Extra Helpful

Folks who do any of the following will be counted as a cut above (and eventually rewarded in some way):

  • Screen capture of you playing
  • Video of your face while you're playing
  • Live journal stream of conciousness of your experience
  • Find a way to reproduce a bug

Goals

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

This page just talks about the bigger, less concrete ideas that drive the game. Feel free to skip this one.


Two Wolves


There are two major drivers for the design of this game. On the one hand, I want to create a game with combat that feels as tangible and involved as Collosseum : Road to Freedom. On the other, I want to create a sanctuary for my people.

In many shows, movies, and games, you'll find scenes of someone heroically and viscerally using what's at hand to overcome a dicey situation. They'll grab a gun off a fallen enemy or dramatically pull out a hidden weapon to turn the tables - just in time.

Megaman Battle Network took this feeling and ran with it. It was about an operator in the real world feeding equipments to a cyberspace Net Navi, who would utilize that equipment to overcome just exactly the situation that gear was suited for. The feeling of changing tools, adapting to the situation. There's also the struggle when you don't have the right tool for the job.

I want to capture those feelings with the combat. I want items to feel like physical parts of the world. When you're hit in the head, I want you to feel the value and weight of your helmet. When you block with your weapon, I want you to feel that tension of figuring something else out before it breaks. I want you to adapt and overcome. I want you to overcome insurmountable odds with your trusty sidearm and the handful of stuff you happened to find today.

I also really love those moments in anime when somebody makes a narrow dodge and cuts down their opponent in a dramatic finishing blow - or dies suddenly instead. That feeling of "going for it!" and slipping past. Gambling with your life so that your comrades might continue. I want to capture this feeling in the combat movement of the game. You should feel like you could try to go in for the finish, but you might also die spectacularly. You should never feel like you're gaming the system. You should feel like every fight will be decisive.

The Lucky Crab


When I say "a sanctuary for my people", I'm talking about a class of gamers that prefers to overlay roleplay ontop of everything else they're doing in a video game - without sacrificing gameplay or roleplay. This is the person who turns a disconnect into a narcalepsy spell. It's the person who can seamlessly go from talking about the latest happenings in the war to helping you debug your computer, without any ridiculous OOC tags. It's the guy who insists that some random NPC is lying because his character believes it. He does the same quest as you but has a totally different story to tell.

The final form of Chronicles of Delonda will be a game about narratives. Each in-game day, every player will have access to their own Chronicle. In this chronicle, they can record happenings. At a regular time interval at night, all the chronicles will be "submitted". If a player has set their chronicles to public, their entries will be searchable via keywords or player name. If they have not, well, that's just for them to enjoy. The idea is to record your personal experiences and random unfounded beliefs about the game, based on your character and the things you've experienced.

If today you put a slug in the stew and got night sight - put that in the chronicle. Is it true that slugs grant night sight? Who knows! However, the next guy to find a slug can look in the logs for the keyword "slug", to see what they might be useful for. They'll see your post about it. They might try it. They might decide you're an idiot or a genius. They might read your in-character ramblings about how you hate slugs. One guy might focus on cooking recipes and establish himself as a reliable source, a master chef. Each server will have a different personality. The logs will be the natural result of your people playing and discovering a game together.

Camp Chores & Exploration


If you've been around at all, you've heard me say this game is really about camp chores. The reason I say that is that it's the opportunity to explore the world and use your cool combat abilities for non-combat things. None of the camp chores are required. All of them are encouraged. There will probably be too many for everyone to do all of them every night.

That's the idea, though. The idea is that you get together with your bros and optionally improve your chances on the following day. One guy gathers firewood, another preps the food, a third uses the rocks you've found to add fire damage to your weapon. You'll need that because tomorrow you enter the ice nation.

Using the simple task of collecting firewood as an example, I'll try to illustrate what makes it rad. You could use your default mainhand to walk around bopping trees until they fall. You could use its "Slashing" tagged ability to drop those trees faster. Even better still, you could equip some claws and spam your spinny attack to clear a lot of trees at once. One of the people in the party wil be best suited to each task and they'll get to flex their defaults to do it. This is the time to live out the identity of the character you've created over all the previous steps.

Once you have the firewood, the cook can get his cook on. The smith can fire up his forge. Each one yielding to the one best suited. Each one increasing the efficacy of the party as a whole. All the while, avoiding the dangers of the ever-encroaching dark. This is when you become a party.

This stage of preparing : it can be as involved or as simple as the party wants. You could skip food entirely and just go without temporary HP. You could skip smithing and get by without typed damage bonus.

However, if you're going to be taking on a world boss, you can also spend multiple in-game days getting ready. You can plan a route that will yield the resources you might need for specific modifications (that are based on your people's collective knowledge about the game). This route also needs to take you to the world boss in a timely manner. Should one party member fall, they'll have to catch back up with limited resources. It's a true march on the enemy and it's fueled by camp chores all along the way.

When you log off, it's all gone. You may have unlocked some nifty new defaults or stacked up some bits for next time, but the world will be there waiting. You won't be missing anything. You won't need to do anything before jumping back in. You won't have to remember where you left off. You'll just call up some bros and strike out into the world to write your own story.




This is the tangibility I want to achieve. When you've got a legendary item, it should feel like it has a presence in the world - both in and out of combat.

Known Bugs & Jank

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

Keyboard controls can get a bit whack if a controller is plugged in. Unplug any controllers, if you find yourself stutter-stepping.

Currently there basic character movement code is extremely limited. You can't go over even the tiniest of ledges. You'll just have to jump.

It's relatively easy to jump off test_room2 and fall forever.

There's significant slowdown in test_room when dudes spawn.

Enemies outside 50 units distance of any player may just chill in the air until you approach. This is the result of a stop-gap method to reduces physics load for slower computers.

There is no P v P at the moment.

Many of the animations expose underlying skin. Joints bend weird. Items clip.

If you find bugs and jank outside of this, please do report it.

Suggestions

Created Thursday 23 June 2022

Suggestions differ from feedback in some important ways. Feedback is about responding to what you've been given. Suggestions are about improving what you're being given.

Not all suggestions are in line with the goals that I'm pursing for the game. Some stuff is down to total personal preference. Please do not be offended if your suggestion is never implemented. I'll do my best to be honest about the chances.

What is a good suggestion?

Suggestions should be accompanied by an explanation of how they will improve the game. It's easy to say "we should have world pvp!". It's entirely another to explain how world pvp helps us to achieve the goals. Almost certainly, whatever vision of the game you are pursuing with your suggestion is not the same one I'm after. However, maybe yours is better! You have to help me see it, if you want your suggestions to get anywhere. At least one change has already been made as a result of suggestions backed by reasons. Why not yours next?
